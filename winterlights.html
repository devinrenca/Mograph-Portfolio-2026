The Shelburne Museum

360° Animation Exhibit

Roles: Art Direction, Animation, Sound Design

This exhibit uses 360° video to incorporate digital animation into the Winter Lights at the Shelburne Museum. Using QR codes scattered around the grounds, the three fantastical sequences could be viewed on visitor's mobile phones during the event.

The Winter Lights at the Shelburne Museum is a landmark of the Vermont winter landscape.

During the event, the Museum is illuminated by dozens of light displays that transform the grounds into a twinkling winter wonderland.

For the 2024/25 season, the Museum reached out to incorporate animation into their famed Winter Lights event. We settled on an approach that used QR codes scattered throughout the grounds to launch 360° videos on visitor's mobile phones. Using animation, these 360° videos digitally transformed the three sites throughout the grounds in unexpected ways, providing a unique perspective for the event.

During the project process, I captured 360° imagery of the grounds. Animation was then added over the imagery. The 360° functionality allowed user to move their phones, changing perspective during each performance.
